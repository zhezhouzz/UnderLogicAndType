# Open / Relevant Normalization Audit

Status: active.  This file tracks remaining proof-facing wrappers and raw-open
forms in `Denotation`, `ContextTyping`, and `ContextBasicDenotation`.

Policy:

- `relevant_lvars` / `relevant_env` should be parsing abbreviations, not
  proof-facing definitions.
- Ordinary Denotation/Soundness helper statements should use direct support
  forms such as `context_ty_lvars Ï„ âˆª tm_lvars e` and
  `lty_env_restrict_lvars Î£ (...)`.
- Raw `formula_open`, `lty_env_open_one (typed_lty_env_bind ...)`,
  `cty_open (cty_shift ...)`, and unnormalized `qual_open_atom` may remain in
  syntax/normalizer lemmas, but should not leak into high-level helper
  premises.

## Iteration 1: Source Relevant Definitions

Problem:

- `RelevantEnv.v` still defines `relevant_lvars` and `relevant_env` as real
  constants.
- This lets upper-layer helper statements keep wrapper names and encourages
  proofs to use `unfold relevant_lvars` / `unfold relevant_env`.

Action:

- Convert `relevant_lvars` and `relevant_env` to parsing abbreviations.
- Replace local `unfold relevant_*` proof steps with direct unfolding of
  `lty_env_restrict_lvars` plus support normalization.

Status: deferred after inspection.

Attempting to turn the source definitions into notations immediately exposes a
large amount of existing proof script that still performs `unfold relevant_*`.
This should be done, but not with a single mechanical replacement.  Keep this
as a source-level goal and first reduce the largest upper-layer public/helper
statements so the eventual source change is smaller.
