LC Premise Cleanup Analysis
===========================

Goal
----
Scan Denotation/Soundness/BasicDenotation/Logic/TypeLanguage from lower layers
upward and remove LC premises that are derivable from stronger regular
hypotheses.  Keep LC premises when they are genuinely primitive syntax side
conditions for open/shift/evaluation lemmas.

Pass 1: Denotation term equivalence layer
-----------------------------------------

Finding: `tm_fiber_equiv_open_app_fvar`

  File: `Denotation/theories/TypeEquivTermApp.v`

  The explicit premises

    lc_tm e1 ->
    lc_tm e2 ->

  are NOT redundant from the stated assumptions.  The premise
  `tm_fiber_equiv_on m X e1 e2` only states equality of result sets inside
  each `X` fiber:

    forall σ0, σ0 ∈ res_restrict m X ->
      forall v, tm_fiber_result_on m X e1 σ0 v <->
                tm_fiber_result_on m X e2 σ0 v

  It does not imply `lc_tm e1` or `lc_tm e2`.

  The related stronger premise `typed_fiber_equiv_on Σ τ m e1 e2` DOES imply
  `lc_tm e1 /\ lc_tm e2`, via `typed_fiber_equiv_term_lc`, because it includes
  two `ty_denote_gas 0` guard facts.

  Status: keep the LC premises on `tm_fiber_equiv_open_app_fvar`.

Candidates for cleanup
----------------------

1. Denotation `TypeEquivArrow*.v` / `TypeEquivWand*.v`:
   lemmas with both `typed_total_equiv_on Σ τ m e1 e2` and explicit
   `lc_tm e1` / `lc_tm e2` can often derive LC by
   `typed_total_equiv_term_lc`.

2. Denotation `TypeEquivFiber*.v`:
   lemmas with `typed_fiber_equiv_on Σ τ m e1 e2` and explicit term LC can
   often derive LC by `typed_fiber_equiv_term_lc`.

3. Soundness files:
   LC facts derived from `context_typing_wf` should be generated by
   `context_typing_regular` or the specific regular lemmas, not repeated as
   caller-facing lemma premises.  Proof-local assertions may remain when they
   are semantic checkpoints, but repeated LC assertions should be replaced by
   regular extraction.

Pass 2: Remaining LC premise classes
------------------------------------

Lower syntax/evaluation layers:

  `ContextBasicDenotation/theories/TermEval.v` and the term-transport files
  still expose many `lc_tm` / `lc_value` premises.  These are generally not
  redundant: lemmas such as `tm_must_terminating_agree_on_fv`,
  `tm_eval_in_store_ret_value_inv`, open/substitution commuting, and
  application/result transport need LC as a primitive syntax condition.  No
  stronger regular hypothesis is present in those statements.

Denotation typed transport:

  If a lemma has `typed_total_equiv_on Σ τ m e1 e2`, LC is derivable by
  `typed_total_equiv_term_lc`.

  If a lemma has `typed_fiber_equiv_on Σ τ m e1 e2`, LC is derivable by
  `typed_fiber_equiv_term_lc`.

  A second scan found the remaining explicit `lc_tm` premises in this area are
  either:

    - on lower-level term lemmas that do not carry a typed equivalence premise;
    - on open-normalization lemmas where LC is the actual rewrite side
      condition;
    - or on value/result-alias lemmas where the value LC is not implied by the
      result relation alone.

Soundness:

  LC facts derived from `context_typing_wf` remain proof-local when they feed
  syntax/eval lemmas directly.  The result-env movement helpers no longer
  require caller-facing LC terms.

Current status:

  The obvious redundant LC premises found in Denotation/Soundness have been
  removed.  Remaining LC premises appear to be either primitive syntax
  conditions or derivable only in callers that already use regular extraction.
