Duplicate Proof Analysis
========================

Scope
-----

This file tracks repeated proof bodies and repeated proof shapes in:

- `Denotation/theories/*.v`
- `ContextTyping/theories/Soundness*.v`

The goal is not to delete lemmas. When a lemma name is useful locally, keep it
as a compatibility wrapper around a more general or lower-level fact.

Initial Scan
------------

The scanner found 709 proof blocks.

Exact repeated proof bodies:

- 5 copies of the `lc_lvars (lvars_shift_from k D)` proof:
  - `ResultFirstOpen.result_first_lc_lvars_shift_from`
  - `TypeEquivWandResultFirst.wand_lc_lvars_shift_from`
  - `TypeEquivFiberBody.lc_lvars_shift_from`
  - `TypeEquivArrowResultFirst.arrow_lc_lvars_shift_from`
  - `TypeDenote.denotation_open_lvars_shift_from_lc`
- 3 copies of the `lvars_shift_from k D = D` proof under `lc_lvars D`:
  - `TypeEquivWandResultFirst.wand_lvars_shift_from_lc_eq`
  - `TypeEquivFiberBody.lvars_shift_from_lc_eq`
  - `TypeEquivArrowResultFirst.arrow_lvars_shift_from_lc_eq`
- Several small zero-denotation projections are duplicated between
  `TypeEquivBody` and `TypeEquivFiberBaseCore`.
- Arrow/Wand result-first files contain many symmetric wrappers.

Repeated proof shapes:

- `formula_scoped_in_world ...` assertions in result-first Arrow/Wand/TLet and
  persistence proofs.
- `store_closed (store_restrict σ X)` in term transport and result extension
  proofs.
- raw opened `formula_open ...` result-first assertions in Arrow/Wand/TLet.
- world-domain membership facts such as `x ∈ world_dom m` and
  `y ∈ world_dom my`.
- repeated FV/fresh side conditions around `relevant_env` and inserted lty
  environments.

Optimization Rounds
-------------------

Round 1: `lvars_shift_from` wrappers.

- Keep the canonical proof in `ResultFirstOpen`.
- Replace local duplicate proofs in ResultFirst/Fiber files with wrappers.
- Do not change `TypeDenote.denotation_open_lvars_shift_from_lc` in this round,
  because `ResultFirstOpen` depends on `TypeDenote`.
- Status: completed for `TypeEquivFiberBody`,
  `TypeEquivArrowResultFirst`, and `TypeEquivWandResultFirst`.
  `ResultFirstOpen` now also exposes
  `result_first_lvars_shift_from_lc_eq`.

Round 2: Arrow/Wand inserted-result basic typing helpers.

- `TypeEquivArrow.basic_value_has_ltype_arrow_inserted_result_target_arg`
  and `TypeEquivArrow.basic_tm_has_ltype_arrow_inserted_result_target_fun`
  were already generic in their statements, despite the names.
- They are now exported and reused by the Wand-local wrappers.
- Status: completed. This removed the duplicate 7-line and 39-line Wand
  proof bodies without changing caller-facing Wand lemma names.

Round 3: Arrow/Wand result body relevant-variable subset.

- Added `ResultFirstOpen.result_first_result_body_relevant_subset`, a generic
  lemma saying the opened result-body application only observes the top type
  after removing the fresh function and argument slots.
- Replaced `arrow_result_body_relevant_subset` and
  `wand_result_body_relevant_subset` by wrappers.
- Status: completed. This removed the duplicate 31-line proof body.

  wrappers over those bottom-layer facts.
- `TypeDenote.denotation_open_lvars_shift_from_lc` is also now a wrapper over
  `ContextBase.lvars_shift_from_lc`.

Deferred Candidates
-------------------

- Move or merge zero-denotation projections only after checking whether both
  files still need separate import boundaries.
- Result-first `formula_scoped_in_world` assertions should be extracted only
  together with the open-normalization boundary; otherwise the helper
  statements become harder to read.
- Large Fix proofs are semantic induction proofs and should be split only by
  meaningful induction stages, not by line count.
