Lemma lowering pass, top down
================================

Scope and policy
----------------

This pass scans from the upper proof layers downwards.  For each folder, the
goal is not to delete useful API lemmas; it is to identify proofs in upper
files that are really Store/Resource/Term/erasure facts and move those facts to
the lowest natural layer.  A lemma is a lowering candidate when:

- the proof body repeatedly unfolds Store/World/Resource machinery in an upper
  file;
- the statement is independent of the upper theorem being proved, or can be
  made independent by replacing local names with semantic parameters;
- the same proof pattern appears in more than one upper proof, or the current
  upper proof is long only because it rebuilds a lower-level fact.

Current branch: codex/denotation-soundness-pattern-refactor.

ContextTyping/theories
----------------------

Hot files:

- SoundnessApp.v
- SoundnessAppD.v
- SoundnessLam.v
- Soundness.v

Findings:

1. SoundnessApp.v, app_arrow_result_to_target

   The proof contains a pure term-evaluation equivalence block:

     tm_equiv_on m
       (tapp_tm (tret v1) (vfvar x))
       (tapp v1 (vfvar x))

   It proves the equivalence by restricting every store to the free variables
   of the two terms and then applying tm_eval_in_store_tapp_tm_ret_equiv.  This
   block is independent of App soundness except for the closedness/lc premises.
   It belongs in ContextBasicDenotation/theories/TermEval.v.

   Planned lowering:

     tm_equiv_on_tapp_tm_ret

   Suggested statement:

     Lemma tm_equiv_on_tapp_tm_ret m v1 x :
       lc_value v1 ->
       wfworld_closed_on
         (fv_tm (tapp_tm (tret v1) (vfvar x)) ∪
          fv_tm (tapp v1 (vfvar x))) m ->
       tm_equiv_on m
         (tapp_tm (tret v1) (vfvar x))
         (tapp v1 (vfvar x)).

   After this, app_arrow_result_to_target should consume the guard-derived
   closedness fact and call the lower-level lemma.

2. SoundnessAppD.v, appd_arg_singleton_env_to_wand_arg

   This proof still performs open/relevant-env normalization and singleton
   argument extraction.  It is AppD-specific at the statement level, but the
   middle pattern is a useful denotation fact:

     a closed wand argument denotation restricted to {[x]} can be converted to
     the opened FBWand argument formula.

   The pure singleton-restriction subpart does not depend on AppD.  It has been
   lowered to Denotation/theories/TypeEquiv.v as:

     ty_denote_gas_restrict_ret_fvar_closed

   AppD still owns the formula_open/FBWand argument normalization because that
   part is case-specific.

3. Soundness.v, ordinary Let/LetD support

   The remaining helper lemmas here are case-level semantic adapters:
   ctx_bind_of_result_ext, ctx_bind_from_result_ext, ty_denote_under_* insert
   and drop facts.  They use Denotation/Context and TypeEquiv APIs rather than
   proving raw Store facts directly.  No immediate lowering candidate in this
   pass.

4. SoundnessLam.v

   The large Lam/LamD helpers mostly normalize Arrow/Wand denotation and then
   use TypeEquivArrow/TypeEquivWand facts.  There are still long statements,
   but they are semantic case adapters rather than raw lower-level proofs.
   Keep them in the soundness support layer for now.

Denotation/theories
-------------------

Hot files:

- Context.v
- TypeEquivCore.v
- TypeEquivArrow.v
- TypeEquivWand.v
- TypeDenote.v

Findings:

1. Context.v, atom_env_to_lty_env_ctx_erasure_under_comma_union and
   atom_env_to_lty_env_ctx_erasure_under_star_union

   These proofs are long and contain many raw map/store lookup cases.  They are
   not pure Store facts because they mention ctx_fv, erase_ctx, ctx_dom, and
   basic_ctx.  The right lowering is not to ContextStore, but to a local raw
   erasure equality helper inside Context.v, separating:

   - a ctx-specific domain/freshness setup;
   - a reusable store equality over A/E1/E2.

   Candidate helper:

     store_union_restrict_ctx_comma_partition
     store_union_restrict_ctx_star_partition

   These can be stated over raw stores A E1 E2 and finite sets F1/F2/D1 plus
   the domain/freshness side conditions.  They should eliminate the repeated
   map_lookup_union/storeA_restrict destruct blocks.

   This is a medium-risk cleanup because both equalities are used by basic
   world reconstruction for ctx_denote_under; defer until after the low-risk
   TermEval lowering.

2. TypeEquivCore.v

   Large proofs such as tm_equiv_tlete_body_extension and result extension
   typing are semantic facts already at the right layer.  No pure Store
   lowering candidate found in this pass.

3. TypeEquivArrow.v / TypeEquivWand.v

   Many proofs still solve singleton/fresh FV side conditions manually.  Most
   of those are candidates for normalizer/solver improvement, not lemma
   lowering.  Existing helper APIs are semantic: opened argument/result guards,
   target zero guards, and tm-equivalence recursive packages.

4. TypeDenote.v

   The formula_open_env_ty_denote_gas proof is necessarily structural over
   context_ty and stays here.  The short FV lemmas
   fv_tm_ty_denote_gas_subset_formula and
   relevant_env_fv_ty_denote_gas_subset_formula are currently unused, but they
   are direct denotation formula API facts, not wrappers; keep them.
