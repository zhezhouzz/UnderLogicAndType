Denotation / Typing quality refactor report
==========================================

Scope
-----

This report covers the current branch:

  codex/denotation-quality-refactor

Folders inspected:

  Denotation/theories
  ContextTyping/theories

The intended cleanup rule is:

  - set_solver is allowed.
  - better_set_solver / better_map_solver / better_store_solver / better_base_solver are encouraged.
  - What should disappear is naked proof-script use of concrete stdpp/base/store
    lemmas such as elem_of_union, elem_of_map, lookup_insert, lookup_union_r,
    storeA_restrict_lookup_some, storeA_restrict_dom, atom_store_to_lvar_store_dom,
    lvars_fv_union, etc.

High-level findings
-------------------

The biggest remaining patterns are not raw set_solver calls. They are:

  1. Repeated concrete set/map/store lemma chains around relevant environments,
     store restriction, and atom-to-lvar stores.
  2. Local hand normalization of formula/type/term lvars with repeated
     lvars_fv_union, lvars_fv_singleton_free/bound, lvars_of_atoms, elem_of_map.
  3. Definitions that are thin wrappers around already-clear core definitions.
  4. Lemmas that are either zero-use after the direct Soundness cleanup, or are
     only one-step wrappers and can be inlined or moved into a solver/normalizer.
  5. Proof scripts that introduce large assert packets for facts that should be
     derivable by a small reusable lemma or a better solver.

Important existing automation
-----------------------------

Already available:

  ContextBase/Tactics.v
    - set_normalize_step
    - set_normalize
    - better_set_solver
    - better_map_solver

  ContextStore/Store.v
    - store_normalize
    - better_store_solver

  ContextBase/BaseTactics.v
    - better_base_solver

The next refactor should mainly strengthen these solvers and then replace
concrete lemma chains by calls to these solvers. It should not try to remove
plain set_solver when it is the right terminal solver.
