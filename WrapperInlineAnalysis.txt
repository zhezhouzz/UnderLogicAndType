Wrapper Inline Analysis
=======================

Goal
----
Inline local helper lemmas whose statements are much larger than their proof
body, are used only once, and do not mark a real semantic boundary. Keep
transport/normalization lemmas that name reusable proof ideas.

Batch 1: TypeEquivTLet.v
------------------------

Inline:

- `tlet_intro_denotation_arrow_body`
- `tlet_intro_denotation_arrow_body_reverse`
- `tlet_intro_denotation_wand_body`
- `tlet_intro_denotation_wand_body_reverse`

Reason:

- Each has a long statement exposing result-first Arrow/Wand bodies.
- Each proof only reconstructs `typed_fiber_equiv_tlet_body_extension`,
  projects it, and applies the corresponding result-first body transport.
- Each is used exactly once.
- The `IH` argument is unused, which is a strong signal that the lemma is only
  forwarding local context.

Keep:

- `tlet_arrow_result_first_body_transport`
- `tlet_wand_result_first_body_transport`

Reason:

- These are the real reusable semantic transport facts for result-first
  Arrow/Wand bodies.

Status
------

- Batch 1 implemented.

Batch 2: SoundnessLamArrow.v
----------------------------

Inline:

- `lam_body_to_opened_arrow_result`

Reason:

- Single call site.
- Proof only composes `lam_body_to_arrow_result_mid` with
  `lam_arrow_result_mid_to_opened_goal`.
- The two composed lemmas are the reusable semantic steps; the middle wrapper
  only repeats a long opened-arrow statement.

Deferred:

- `lam_arrow_result_mid_app_guard`: long statement and short final proof
  shape, but the body contains substantial guard/total/equivalence reasoning.
  Keeping it avoids inlining a large proof into the main lambda case.
- `lam_arrow_result_mid_app_zero`: short wrapper, but named zero-denotation
  conversion is useful and still has multiple nearby callers.
